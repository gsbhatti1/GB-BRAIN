## Risk Analysis

Despite the advantages of the RSI and EMA Dual Filter Strategy, there are still some potential risks:

1. Trend Reversal Risk: When the market trend changes, the EMA lines may lag behind, causing the strategy to miss optimal entry or exit points.
2. Parameter Optimization Risk: The performance of the strategy is highly sensitive to parameter settings; different combinations of parameters can result in vastly different outcomes. Over-optimization of parameters could lead to poor future market performance.
3. Black Swan Event Risk: The strategy relies on historical data for backtesting and optimization, but past data cannot fully account for extreme events that might occur in the future. Such events may significantly impact the strategy's returns.

To address these risks, consider implementing the following solutions:

1. Combine with other technical indicators or price behavior patterns to assist in trend reversal judgments, allowing for timely adjustments.
2. Use moderate parameter optimization and avoid overfitting historical data excessively. Regularly review and adjust parameters to adapt to new market characteristics.
3. Set reasonable stop-loss levels to control maximum single-trade losses. Additionally, implement risk management at the portfolio level through strategies like diversification and position sizing.
