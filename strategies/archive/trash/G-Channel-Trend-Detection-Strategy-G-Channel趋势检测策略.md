#### Overview
The G-Channel Trend Detection Strategy is a quantitative trading strategy based on the G-channel indicator. The strategy calculates the upper and lower extremes of the G-channel and determines the current market trend by the crossover between price and the G-channel moving average, generating buy and sell signals accordingly. Additionally, it sets take profit and stop loss conditions to control risk.

#### Strategy Principle
1. Calculate the upper and lower extremes \(a\) and \(b\), where \(a\) is the difference between the historical high price and the previous period's \(a\) value divided by the period length, and \(b\) is the sum of the historical low price plus the difference between the previous period's \(a\) value and \(b\) value divided by the period length.
2. Calculate the G-channel moving average \(avg\), i.e., \(\frac{(a+b)}{2}\).
3. Determine the crossover situation between the price and the \(b\) value. If the price crosses above the \(b\) value, it is considered a bullish trend; if the price crosses below the \(a\) value, it is considered a bearish trend.
4. In a bullish trend, if the previous candle is bearish and the current candle turns bullish, a buy signal is generated; in a bearish trend, if the previous candle is bullish and the current candle turns bearish, a sell signal is generated.
5. Set take profit and stop loss conditions. When holding a long position, the take profit price is the buy price multiplied by \((1 + \text{take profit percentage})\), and the stop loss price is the buy price multiplied by \((1 - \text{stop loss percentage})\); when holding a short position, the take profit price is the sell price multiplied by \((1 - \text{take profit percentage})\), and the stop loss price is the sell price multiplied by \((1 + \text{stop loss percentage})\).

#### Strategy Advantages
1. The G-channel indicator can effectively capture market trends, generating buy and sell signals based on the crossover between the price and the G-channel moving average, making it simple and easy to use.
2. Take profit and stop loss settings can effectively control risk, preventing excessive losses from a single trade.
3. The strategy logic is clear and easy to understand and implement, suitable for beginners in quantitative trading.

#### Strategy Risks
1. The G-channel indicator may generate more false signals during market fluctuations, leading to frequent trading and high slippage costs.
2. The setting of take profit and stop loss percentages needs to be adjusted according to market characteristics and personal risk preferences; inappropriate parameter settings can lead to poor strategy returns.
3. The strategy does not consider the specifics of the traded asset, such as suspension of trading or price limit ups and downs in stock strategies, which require further optimization.

#### Strategy Optimization Directions
1. Other technical indicators, such as ATR and RSI, can be introduced to conduct secondary confirmation of signals generated by the G-channel indicator, improving signal reliability.
2. For take profit and stop loss percentages, a dynamic adjustment approach can be adopted based on market volatility and holding time, enhancing strategy adaptability.
3. Based on the characteristics of the traded asset, corresponding risk control modules can be added; for example, in stock strategies, handling logic can be set for special situations like trading suspensions or price limit ups and downs.

#### Summary
The G-Channel Trend Detection Strategy is a simple quantitative trading strategy based on the G-channel indicator that generates buy and sell signals by capturing market trends and sets take profit and stop loss conditions to control risk. The strategy logic is clear and easy to implement, making it suitable for beginners in quantitative trading. However, this strategy may generate more false signals in fluctuating markets, and the take profit and stop loss percentages need to be adjusted according to market characteristics. Additionally, it does not consider the specifics of the traded asset. Future improvements can include introducing other technical indicators, dynamically adjusting take profit and stop loss percentages, and adding risk control modules based on the characteristics of the traded asset to enhance strategy stability and profitability.
